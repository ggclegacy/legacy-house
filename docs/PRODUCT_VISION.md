# Legacy House Product Vision

## Mission

Legacy House is Groomed Gent Co.'s private Product Intelligence OS: one trustworthy operational memory for taking products from founder intent through research, sourcing, production, quality, launch, and portfolio decisions. It should connect evidence and decisions across the lifecycle rather than imitate a generic CRUD back office.

## Product lines

- **Legacy Reserve:** grooming and related products, including the supplied canonical Hair & Beard Oil formula.
- **Legacy Sanctum:** wellness product planning, initially through a white-label development path.
- **Future Groomed Gent Co. lines:** supported through reusable product-line configuration rather than hardcoded duplicate systems.

The four permanent pillars are CREATE (research and R&D), BUILD (sourcing and commercialization), CONTROL (inventory, production, traceability, and quality), and SCALE (launch, commerce performance, forecasting, and decisions). They organize product thinking but need not become literal navigation labels.

## Core users

- **Founder/product decision-maker:** sets briefs, evaluates evidence, approves direction, and reviews portfolio health.
- **Product/R&D operator:** manages concepts, formula versions, experiments, ingredients, and conclusions.
- **Sourcing/operations operator:** manages vendors, quotes, packaging, purchase orders, receiving, inventory, production, and costs.
- **Quality/compliance operator:** manages specifications, holds, stability, label/claim evidence, release, changes, and complaints.
- **Analyst/leadership reader:** reviews launches, performance, demand, risk, and recommendations without altering controlled records.

Authorization must be explicit even when one person initially fills multiple roles. External vendors and customers are not initial direct users.

## Major workflows and lifecycle

1. Capture sourced research, an opportunity, and a product brief.
2. Choose a development path: custom formula, white/private label, or manufacturer-developed.
3. For custom products, version formulas and execute documented experiments; for white-label products, record vendor facts without fabricating internal formulas.
4. Qualify ingredients, suppliers, manufacturers, packaging, samples, quotes, specifications, costs, and risks.
5. Approve launch readiness and plan purchasing, receiving, production, inventory, traceability, and quality controls.
6. Preserve exact production and cost history; separate completion, quality disposition, and release.
7. Manage labels, claims, stability evidence, changes, and complaints with provenance.
8. Launch through controlled readiness gates; initially read Shopify data without writing back.
9. Turn real operational and market history into forecasts, exception queues, and founder decisions.

Every transition should retain who, when, why, supporting evidence, and the prior state where the record is controlled.

## Long-term intelligence vision

Legacy House should eventually explain what is true, what changed, what needs attention, and what evidence supports a decision. Intelligence can connect product readiness, lead time, available stock, quality status, costs, launch performance, and demand. Forecasts must expose input periods, assumptions, confidence/accuracy, and overrides. Future AI may summarize records, surface anomalies, compare sourced options, and draft decision support; it must respect permissions, cite records, distinguish facts from inference, and require humans for controlled approvals.

## Non-goals

- A public storefront, customer portal, social network, generic ERP clone, electronic lab notebook for every scientific discipline, or replacement for legal/regulatory professionals.
- Invented demo business data presented as real.
- Premature microservices, broad AI automation, Shopify write-back, or autonomous quality/regulatory decisions.
- Treating visual polish as a substitute for trustworthy workflows and data.

## Success criteria

- A user can trace an active product from brief and development path through its sourced decisions, approved records, production/quality history, launch, and measured outcomes.
- Controlled history is reproducible: formula, lot, transaction, cost, evidence, decision, and status changes are attributable and not silently rewritten.
- Users see unknowns and exceptions clearly and can distinguish actual, quoted, estimated, calculated, and forecast data.
- Critical workflows work accessibly across mobile and desktop and are deployable reproducibly on Vercel with isolated environments and fail-closed data access.
- The system reduces duplicate records and manual reconciliation while improving readiness, risk visibility, and decision speed.
